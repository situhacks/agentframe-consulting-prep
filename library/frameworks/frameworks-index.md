# Frameworks — Quick Reference

One-line descriptions to name and pick the right framework during a case. Pick a framework to FIT the problem;
don't force a canned one. (Distilled from gcamilo/management-consulting — see README for credit.)

## Problem solving (the backbone)
- **MECE** — mutually exclusive, collectively exhaustive decomposition.
- **Issue tree** — problem → MECE sub-problems → data needed at each leaf.
- **Hypothesis tree** — problem → MECE hypotheses → kill tests (try to disprove).
- **Pyramid principle** — answer FIRST, then supporting arguments, then evidence.
- **7-step** — define · disaggregate · prioritize · plan · analyze · synthesize · recommend.

## Profitability & sizing (most common case types)
- **Profit tree** — Revenue (Price × Volume) − Cost (Fixed + Variable). The workhorse for "profits are down".
- **Revenue tree** — segments × products × channels × pricing.
- **Market sizing (TAM/SAM/SOM)** — top-down or bottoms-up; state assumptions, show the logic.
- **Unit economics** — LTV, CAC, payback, contribution margin.
- **Waterfall / bridge** — incremental contributions to a total.

## Strategy & market
- **3 Cs** — Customer, Competitor, Company. Great for market/landscape overview.
- **4 Ps** — Product, Price, Place, Promotion. For "how to grow share" / go-to-market.
- **Five Forces** (Porter) — industry attractiveness via 5 competitive pressures.
- **Value chain** — primary + support activities; find cost/differentiation advantage.
- **Ansoff** — growth via market penetration / market dev / product dev / diversification.
- **Growth-share (BCG) matrix** — Stars / Cash Cows / Dogs / Question Marks.

## Decision making
- **Decision matrix** — weighted criteria across options.
- **Pre-mortem** — assume it failed; work backward to causes.
- **Inversion** — instead of "how to succeed," ask "how could this fail?"
- **Second-order thinking** — consequences of the consequences.

## Operations & change (implementation cases)
- **Customer journey map** — touchpoints, emotions, pain points.
- **Change models** — ADKAR; Kotter's 8 steps; Lewin (unfreeze → change → refreeze).

## Innovation / business model
- **Jobs to Be Done** — what "job" is the customer hiring this product for?
- **Business Model Canvas** — 9 building blocks of a business model.

## Pricing
- **Value-based** (to perceived value) · **Cost-plus** · **Competitive** · **Dynamic** · **Freemium**.
- The three classic pricing methods to walk through: **value-add** (quantify the customer's marginal benefit) ·
  **cost-plus** (your cost + target margin) · **competitive** (what comparable products charge).

## Case-type playbooks (common prompts → what to cover)
- **Market entry (geographic / new product):** Strategic logic (why now — growth? mature market? competitor
  move?) → Resources/capabilities (brand, patents, partners) → Economics (size the market, share, investment,
  **payback / breakeven** — market-entry almost always needs a sizing + breakeven) → Risks (entry barriers,
  regulatory, cannibalization for new products, FX/macro).
- **Outsourcing:** Strategic logic (cost savings? market access?) → Economics (in-house cost vs. outsourced cost
  + transition investment → net savings) → Risks/partner (quality, lead time, stakeholder/job-loss mgmt).
- **M&A:** Strategic fit (cost vs. revenue synergy; vertical/horizontal/diversification) → Economics (basic
  **DCF**: value ≈ after-tax profit ÷ cost of capital; compare synergies + new firm value vs. deal price) →
  Risks (acquisition track record, culture/PMI fit, can investors diversify themselves). Use a
  **synergy matrix** (Firm A × Firm B → A+B across Company / Customers / Competition / Products) when the two
  firms differ a lot.
- **Not-for-profit:** Mission first (not profit) → Stakeholders (donors, beneficiaries, volunteers, staff) →
  Economics (cost, do we have the money, any return) → Risks (media perception, alienating donors/volunteers).
- **Capacity change:** Supply & demand — if conceptual use the framework, if numerical draw the curves. Demand
  (market growth × firm share, segment + focus on largest sources) → Supply (industry supply, productivity/cost
  benefits of the change) → Cost of expansion (can they afford it; payback/breakeven; alternatives:
  outsource / lease / sublet).

> For a case: pick ONE structure that fits, make it MECE, lead with the answer (pyramid principle), and label
> your claims (see `evidence-standards.md`). For the running order of any case, see `case-flow.md`.
