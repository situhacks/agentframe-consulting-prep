# Technology

> Industry prior for case practice - use it to ask sharper questions and sanity-check, NOT as case facts. Solve
> the case with its own data. (From the SFU MCCP industry snapshots.)

**Overview.** Systems (PCs, servers), semiconductors, communications equipment, software, internet, and IT services.

**Key trends.**
- Rising M&A as growth slows, creating one-stop-shop solutions (Oracle/PeopleSoft)
- Co-opetition: rivals also collaborate (Microsoft/Intel, Oracle/IBM)
- Cloud computing as outsourced utility across subsectors

**Competitive landscape.**
- Systems: IBM, HP
- Semis: Intel, Samsung, TI
- Comms: Cisco, Nokia
- Software: Microsoft, Oracle, SAP
- Internet: Google
- IT services: Accenture, IBM

**Customers.**
- By size: Enterprise, SMB, Retail
- By type: business vs. individual consumer

**Channels.**
- Enterprise via direct sales force
- SMB/Retail/Consumer via indirect channels

**Profit model.** Profit varies by subsector: Systems low-margin (COGS mgmt); Semis high CAPEX/cyclical (utilization); Software licensing vs. subscription (high gross margin, high R&D); IT services = staff utilization / revenue per employee; Internet = revenue per click.
