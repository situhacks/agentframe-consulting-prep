# Metals & Mining

> Industry prior for case practice - use it to ask sharper questions and sanity-check, NOT as case facts. Solve
> the case with its own data. (From the SFU MCCP industry snapshots.)

**Overview.** Locating and extracting metal/mineral reserves for jewelry, industrial use, investment. Very capital-intensive (mines, production facilities).

**Key trends.**
- Margins fell from ~25% (2011) to ~4% (2013)
- Gold price set by supply/demand
- Cost-reduction wave: headcount, mine closures until prices recover

**Competitive landscape.**
- Glencore, BHP Billiton, Rio Tinto
- Exploration firms (asset-light: drill rights), then Development, then Production (blue chips)

**Customers.**
- Businesses; metals sold on international regulated markets
- Gold: ~50% investment, ~26% central banks, ~14% tech, ~10% jewelry

**Channels.**
- Sold on regulated commodity markets

**Profit model.** Profit = oz mined x market price - cost. You can't control price, only cost via AISC (all-in sustaining cost). VC: labour, marketing. FC: capital (haul trucks, shovels).
