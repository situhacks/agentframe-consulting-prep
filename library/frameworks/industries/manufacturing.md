# Manufacturing

> Industry prior for case practice - use it to ask sharper questions and sanity-check, NOT as case facts. Solve
> the case with its own data. (From the SFU MCCP industry snapshots.)

**Overview.** Mechanical/physical/chemical transformation of materials into new products. Subsectors: textile, paper, chemical, electronics, transport equipment, machinery.

**Key trends.**
- Highly cyclical in most sectors
- Domestic manufacturing pressured by higher cost structure, driving outsourcing to low-cost regions

**Competitive landscape.**
- GM, Chrysler, Ford, Toyota, Honda (auto)
- Boeing, Airbus (aero)
- GE, Phillips, Siemens

**Customers.**
- Varies: end-consumer or OEM (B2B)
- Auto = consumer; Metal = aero/auto/tooling; Apparel = consumer; Plastics = medical/machinery; Chemicals = pharma/semis

**Channels.**
- Retail (auto, apparel)
- Wholesale / B2B (plastics, chemicals, pharma, metal, machinery, semis, hardware)

**Profit model.** Revenue: diversity of customers, volume, emerging markets, new tech/products, warranty. Cost: outsourcing is the swing factor (labour).
