# Pharmaceuticals

> Industry prior for case practice - use it to ask sharper questions and sanity-check, NOT as case facts. Solve
> the case with its own data. (From the SFU MCCP industry snapshots.)

**Overview.** Branded/originator producers make patent-protected drugs; generics make copy-cats at lower cost once patents expire.

**Key trends.**
- Price competition from generics
- Pressure from insurers & hospital chains to cut prices
- R&D challenge of finding high-revenue drugs
- Patent cliffs (~2010-11) for many large players

**Competitive landscape.**
- Key success factor = products (cancer, cardiovascular, etc.)
- US/Europe/Japan largest; China/India growing
- FDA must approve ALL drugs

**Customers.**
- Doctors who prescribe
- Insurers who pay
- Patients who need them
- Emerging markets: officials may control channel access

**Channels.**
- OTC (no prescription)
- Prescription: hospitals, pharmacies
- B2B: distributors, hospitals, pharmacies

**Profit model.** Revenue: treatment-area size/competition, doctor buy-in, speed-to-market (generics). Cost: VC sales & marketing (doctor visits, studies); FC R&D (discovery, trials), much outsourced now.
