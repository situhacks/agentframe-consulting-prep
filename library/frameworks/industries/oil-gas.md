# Oil & Gas (O&G)

> Industry prior for case practice - use it to ask sharper questions and sanity-check, NOT as case facts. Solve
> the case with its own data. (From the SFU MCCP industry snapshots.)

**Overview.** Extracts, refines, produces, distributes and sells the dominant energy source and by-products.

**Key trends.**
- 2015 price crash (oversupply + weak demand) cut upstream revenue up to 50%, driving big CAPEX/opex/headcount cuts
- Climate pressure reshaping the landscape
- Consolidation (Shell/BG); Iran back in market
- Shifting IOC/NOC reserve access

**Competitive landscape.**
- IOCs: Shell, ExxonMobil, Chevron, Conoco, Total
- NOCs: Saudi Aramco, PetroChina, Gazprom, Sinopec, Petronas

**Customers.**
- Corporations, governments, individuals

**Channels.**
- Upstream: locate/extract crude & gas
- Midstream: process/store/transport (pipelines)
- Downstream: refineries, petrochem, distributors, gas stations

**Profit model.** Revenue: bulk from upstream = sales price x barrels (spot or fixed-rate contract); higher oil price means higher revenue. Cost: large CAPEX, operations (labour), transport, exploration/development.
