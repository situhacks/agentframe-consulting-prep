# Media

> Industry prior for case practice - use it to ask sharper questions and sanity-check, NOT as case facts. Solve
> the case with its own data. (From the SFU MCCP industry snapshots.)

**Overview.** Print, audio and video content generation + dissemination. Primarily advertising-supported; online media disrupting traditional models.

**Key trends.**
- Digitization required heavy capital investment
- Uncertain how media is ultimately consumed (internet/cable/mobile)
- 'Free' content hurts generators but creates new channels

**Competitive landscape.**
- Compete for audience to sell advertising
- A few majors own integrated portfolios (e.g. Disney)

**Customers.**
- Consumers are part of the PRODUCT (audience reach/ratings sell ads)
- Advertisers are the real customers in traditional models; subscribers in subscription models

**Channels.**
- Print: paper + online/mobile
- TV: broadcast/cable/satellite/online
- Movies: theatres, online (Netflix)

**Profit model.** Revenue: advertising + subscriptions (shifting toward subscription for premium). Cost: VC production (staff, tech); FC capital (studios, presses), overhead, marketing.
