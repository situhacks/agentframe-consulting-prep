# Case flow — how a case is run (the MCCP standard)

The three stages every case moves through, plus the habits that separate a strong candidate. This is the
**shared standard**: `run-case` runs the candidate through it; `case-reviewer` grades against it. (Adapted from
the SFU MCCP case guide — the cohort's own method.)

## The three stages

### 1. Open
- **Stall (a beat).** Take a few seconds before diving in — it's fine to think.
- **Verify the facts.** Play back the key points. Separate the **PROBLEM** ("profits are down") from the
  **GOAL** ("increase profits" ≠ "increase sales" — they're different). Don't solve a problem that wasn't asked.
- **Clarify** anything about the business you don't understand — better now than mid-case.
- **Ask for high-level context** before structuring (flag it as high-level so it doesn't look like diving in):
  "Before I structure, any high-level context I should know — how long in business, what the market looks like,
  key competencies?"
- **Structure (~60–90s).** Name the TYPE of problem, pick a fitting framework (don't name-drop "Porter's Five" —
  they want original thinking, not memorized templates), state the key branches AND why each matters (shows
  insight), then lay out the tree and walk the interviewer through it.

### 2. Analyze
"Break it into pieces." The loop:
1. State a **hypothesis** ("I'd hypothesize C is true if A and B hold").
2. Pick a branch of the tree.
3. Identify the key issues in that branch.
4. Ask for data to test it — and **say why** you want it (what it will tell you).
5. Go deeper only **if the data supports it**.
6. Refine the hypothesis as you learn.
7. **Pull out the insight** — the "so what" from each piece of data. This is the part that scores.

### 3. Close
Quick and CEO-friendly — pass the **elevator test (<60s)**:
1. **Tie back to the PROBLEM/ASK** — "You asked us to fix X / choose between X, Y, Z."
2. **Answer first** — lead with the recommendation, not the journey: "We recommend X, for 3 reasons."
3. **Support with case data** — points 1, 2, 3.
4. **Reiterate + next steps** — restate the recommendation, name risks / what you'd analyze with more time.

## Habits that score (coach these)
- **Think out loud.** Silence hides your reasoning — the interviewer is grading how you think.
- **Synthesize as you go.** After each chunk, state the aha and park it; you'll reuse it in the close. Also
  re-groups you when stuck.
- **Numerate.** "Three areas: 1… 2… 3…" — client-friendly and forces MECE.
- **Be hypothesis-driven.** Every curiosity is a hypothesis — say it out loud.
- **Ask for data with a reason**, and always extract the insight from it.
- **Hunt the deltas.** What changed YoY (internal issue) or vs. competitors (market issue)? Look for trends.
- **Consider risks** — PREST: Political, Regulatory, Economic, Social, Tech.
- **Use only case data** — not outside facts you think you know.
- **Market-entry cases almost always need a sizing + breakeven.**
- **Good first cuts:** financial vs. non-financial · cost vs. benefit · internal vs. external · quant vs. qual.

> The structure is the scaffold; the **insight** is the score. A right answer rambled, or a tree with no "so
> what", still loses.
